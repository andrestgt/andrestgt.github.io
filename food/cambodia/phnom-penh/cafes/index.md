# Cafés

---

## Digby's
*Preah Trasak Paem St · Café*
Genuinely excellent café serving some of the best cappuccinos in Phnom Penh alongside very solid international bistro food.
→ [Read the full review](/food/cambodia/phnom-penh/cafes/digbys/)

---

## Feel Good Café
*Street 136 · Café*
Very good coffees at 2.50$ a cappuccino — my first port of call whenever I stay riverside, despite the uncomfortable wooden chairs.
→ [Read the full review](/food/cambodia/phnom-penh/cafes/feel-good-cafe/)

---

## Eric Kayser Quayside
*Sisowath Quay · Bakery, Café*
Some of the best bread available this side of Europe — worth a stop for a coffee and croissant at 2.80$.
→ [Read the full review](/food/cambodia/phnom-penh/cafes/eric-kayser-quayside/)

---

## Tarraju Café
*Street 370 · Café*
Genuinely very good cappuccino at 3$ — just ask them firmly to skip the cinnamon.
→ [Read the full review](/food/cambodia/phnom-penh/cafes/tarraju-cafe/)

---

## La Croisette
*Sisowath Quay · Café*
One of the old-school riverside cafés with the most comfortable seating anywhere along the riverfront — great for coffee and people-watching.
→ [Read the full review](/food/cambodia/phnom-penh/cafes/la-croisette/)

---

## Cam Cup Café
*Old Post Office · Café*
Charming setting in the old post office building with good quality coffee, though preparation needs work.
→ [Read the full review](/food/cambodia/phnom-penh/cafes/cam-cup-cafe/)

---

## Xotique Coffee
*Toul Tom Poung · Café*
Decent coffee undermined by dreadful food — the smoked salmon sandwich was a disgrace.
→ [Read the full review](/food/cambodia/phnom-penh/cafes/xotique-coffee/)

## Cafe Fresco Bakery
*Sisowath Quay · Café*
Once one of the best cafés in Phnom Penh, now dramatically declined — no reason to visit when so many better options exist nearby.
