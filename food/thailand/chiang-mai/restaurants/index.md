# Restaurants

A wide selection of restaurants in Chiang Mai, from Michelin-recommended khao soi shops and Northern Thai classics to Japanese sushi bars, fine dining, and international food.

---

## Ai Din Klin Krok
*Fa Ham · Isaan*
Authentic Isaan street food in comfortable surroundings — properly spicy, no concessions to tourist palates.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/ai-din-klin-krok/)

---


## Auf der Au Garden German Buffet
*Wat Ket · German Buffet*
An absolute nightmare for anyone trying to be on a diet — enormous amounts of rich, heavy food at an unbeatable all-in price.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/auf-der-au-german-buffet/)

---


## Big Big Shabu
*Santitham · Mookata Buffet*
All-you-can-eat mookata for just over 200 baht — good value, decent meat and seafood, and the Thai barbecue concept works very well.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/big-big-shabu/)

---

## Blue Diamond The Breakfast Club
*Si Phum, Old Town · Western, Breakfast*
A Chiang Mai institution for farang breakfasts, still going strong — perfect for avocado lovers, with big portions and good coffee.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/blue-diamond-breakfast-club/)

---

## By Hand Pizza Cafe
*Si Phum, Old Town · Pizza*
Superb wood-fired pizzas in the Old Town — the crust is perfect and the quality is remarkable for the price.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/by-hand-pizza/)

---

## Cherng Doi Roast Chicken
*Suthep, Nimman · Thai, Chicken*
Small portions for the price, but very juicy and well-seasoned chicken — a reasonable option for a chicken fix in the Nimman area.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/cherng-doi-roast-chicken/)

---

## Coconut Shell Thai Food
*Phra Sing, Old Town · Thai*
An easy recommendation among Old Town tourist restaurants — solid Thai classics, fair prices, and beautiful presentation including excellent mango shakes.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/coconut-shell-thai-food/)

---

## Dao Chinese Restaurant
*Chang Phueak · Sichuan*
Don't let the sterile condo lobby setting put you off — this is one of the most recommendable Chinese restaurants in Chiang Mai, with excellent beef noodles and dumplings at very low prices.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/dao-chinese-restaurant/)

---

## Daruma Izakaya
*Chang Phueak · Japanese*
One of Chiang Mai's more reasonably priced izakayas — solid grilled skewers and takoyaki, good atmosphere, a perfectly reasonable stop for beers and Japanese food.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/daruma-izakaya/)

---

## David's Kitchen at 909
*Chang Moi · French-Thai Fine Dining*
A real surprise outside of town — impeccable French-Thai influenced cooking, attentive service, and a reasonably priced wine list. The foie gras is excellent.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/davids-kitchen/)

---

## Deck 1
*Chang Moi · International*
Fancy riverside setting with good views and atmosphere — the food is merely okay and very expensive for what it is.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/deck-1/)

---

## Ginza Sushi Bar
*Suthep, Nimman · Japanese*
One of the more moderately priced sushi bars in Nimman — nothing spectacular, but from soft shell crab to unagi and salmon, everything was tasty.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/ginza-sushi-bar/)

---

## Grill of Punjab
*Si Phum, Old Town · Indian*
Moderately priced Indian restaurant — the samosas and dipping sauces are fine, but the naan was dry and the palak paneer lacked salt on the visit reviewed.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/grill-of-punjab/)

---

## THE HOUSE by Ginger
*Si Phum, Old Town · International, Thai*
Very fancy and super comfortable — one of the best restaurants in Chiang Mai, worth a visit for both the food and the ambiance, even if prices are 50% above average.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/house-by-ginger/)

---

## Huen Jai Yong
*San Kamphaeng · Lanna, Northern Thai*
A beautiful, very popular countryside restaurant specialising in traditional Lanna cuisine — the sausages in particular stood out, and it's well worth a stop if you're in the area.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/huen-jai-yong/)

---

## Huen Muan Jai
*Chang Phueak · Northern Thai*
A lovely old wooden villa with a Bib Gourmand — the service and atmosphere are excellent, but the Michelin-praised larb pla khua was not convincing on the visit reviewed.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/huen-muan-jai/)

---

## Huen Phen
*Si Phum, Old Town · Northern Thai*
Quirky tourist restaurant by night, simple canteen for lunch — the eggplant nam prik and pork cracklings are excellent, though the mango salad was too sugary.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/huen-phen/)

---

## Jasmine
*Chang Moi · Chinese, Dim Sum*
A fading hotel restaurant that remains a decent fallback for dim sum — no atmosphere, not great, but not too expensive either.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/jasmine/)

---

## Khaomao-Khaofang Imaginary Jungle
*Hang Dong · Thai*
A jaw-droppingly gorgeous restaurant south of the city — come for the spectacular tropical setting and pondside terrace, not the fairly standard Thai cooking.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/khaomao-khaofang/)

---


## Khanom Jeen Tai Baan
*Phra Sing, Old Town · Northern Thai*
A few plastic tables under a shady mango tree — the lady owner prepares your mango salad fresh from the tree above. A lovely experience at 35 Bt a plate.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/khanom-jeen-tai-baan/)

---

## Kinphak Café – Vegan Food
*Chang Phueak · Vegan, Thai*
A very good vegan khao soi at 55 Bt and delicious stir-fried shirataki noodles — no hipster surcharges, just honest prices. Highly recommended.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/kinphak-cafe-vegan/)

---


## Lookbua Thai Food Restaurant
*Phra Sing, Old Town · Thai*
One of the better tourist-oriented restaurants in the Old Town — solid, well-prepared Thai standards with good service, and the red duck curry is worth coming back for.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/lookbua/)

---

## Mama at Jing Jai Village
*Chang Phueak · Thai, Noodles*
Top notch chicken noodle soup in the attractive Jing Jai Village — slightly pricier than the average local eatery, but worth it for the clean, modern surroundings.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/mama-jing-jai/)

---

## Muay Khao Soi
*Nimman · Northern Thai*
A neat little budget eatery in pricey Nimman — very good khao soi and nam ngiao at very reasonable prices for the area. Great for a quick meal.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/muay-khao-soi/)

---

## Musashi Sushi Bar
*Si Phum, Old Town · Japanese*
The best sushi bar in Chiang Mai — the salmon sashimi, cut from a whole fresh fish in front of you, may be the best I've ever tasted. Super value for the quality.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/musashi-sushi-bar/)

---

## Number 9 Restaurant
*Si Phum, Old Town · Thai*
A large, long-running eatery catering to tourists — freshly prepared food, huge menu, reasonable prices, and a reliable choice in the backpacker part of the Old Town.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/number-9-restaurant/)

---

## Ochatee
*Haiya · Burmese*
A small Burmese bistro in a quiet neighbourhood south of the moat — the mohinga is one of the best versions of this breakfast soup I've found anywhere, and the atmosphere is genuinely lovely.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/ochatee/)

---

## Okada Izakaya
*Nimman · Japanese*
Attractive, mid-priced izakaya in Nimman — tasty sashimi, yakitori, and rolls, draft Asahi under 100 Bt, very friendly service. Recommended.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/okada-izakaya/)

---

## The Oyster Bar at Rimping Supermarket
*Tha Sala · Oysters, Wine*
Wine at retail prices and delicious fresh oysters — skip the Thai food menu and come here for an affordable glass of wine and some very good oysters.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/oyster-bar-rimping/)

---

## Peppermint Cafe
*Si Phum, Old Town · Thai, Fusion*
A nice, quiet place that's been around for a long time — simple, inexpensive food and good shakes. A reliable, unpretentious stop in the Old Town.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/peppermint-cafe/)

---

## Rajdarbar Indian Restaurant
*Chang Khlan · Indian*
Solid Indian delivery — very good garlic naan and masala gravy, with chicken tikka that could have been juicier. Good value for the price.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/rajdarbar-indian/)

---

## Salsa Kitchen
*Chang Phueak · Mexican*
A slight disappointment — soggy enchiladas, bland chicken, and cheap mozzarella instead of proper cheese. The refried beans were tasty and service was friendly, but Mexican food is hard to do well here.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/salsa-kitchen/)

---

## Somtam Rong Pla Daek Santitham
*Si Phum · Thai, Isaan*
Popular local restaurant with good service and friendly staff — but the duck larb lacked spice and herbs, and the gai yang was reheated rather than freshly grilled.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/somtam-rong-pla-daek/)

---

## Taiwanese Food Jinjiao Dumplings
*Chang Phueak, Santitham · Taiwanese*
A small Taiwanese dumpling place in the Santitham area — a great range of steamed and fried dumplings at very reasonable prices. One of my regular stops in the neighbourhood.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/taiwanese-jinjiao/)

---

## Thaan Aoan
*Phra Sing, Old Town · Thai*
Nice, air-conditioned café in the Old Town — the coffee is actually good (ask for no cinnamon), but the Thai food is toned down to the point of blandness for tourists.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/thaan-aoan/)

---

## Tha Chang Hill
*Hang Dong · Thai, International*
Well-prepared cappuccino and very good Thai bistro food on a pretty veranda above the valley — prices slightly above average, but reasonable. A very good pit stop.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/tha-chang-hill/)

---

## Wine Connection Central Festival
*Fa Ham · Wine Bar, Deli*
The most affordable place for wine and deli food in Thailand — an absolute winner for wine and food lovers who don't want to spend a fortune.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/restaurants/wine-connection/)

---


**Price level note:**
Price levels refer to the approximate cost for **two people** ordering an average meal **without alcoholic drinks**.
