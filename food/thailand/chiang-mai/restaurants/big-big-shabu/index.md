I'm generally not a big fan of buffet-style eating. That has less to do with quality, which can be hit or miss, but is often perfectly fine, and more with the fact that I always feel compelled to try everything and usually leave these places feeling overstuffed. For that reason alone, I rarely go. Unfortunately for me, my better half is a big fan, so I occasionally give in.

To be fair, in Thailand the value is hard to argue with. For a bit over 200 baht, you can eat as much as you like, with a wide selection of meat and seafood. I also like the Thai mookata concept itself: grilling meat and seafood while the juices drip into a broth below, which you then turn into a soup with vegetables and other ingredients. As a concept, it works very well.

This place is one of many similar mookata restaurants in the area, and quality-wise they all seem fairly comparable. The meat and seafood are okay, not great, not bad, but the value is excellent. If you enjoy barbecues or simply want to eat a lot for very little money, this is definitely a place worth considering.
