I had no idea about the hype and fame of this stall when we stumbled upon it on a quiet Friday night looking for a good street food meal. Nor did I know that this is the khao kha moo place that the great Anthony Bourdain came to visit in Chiang Mai.

What I did know immediately on the first bite, though, is that this is probably the best pork stew I've tried so far. The meat is juicy and tender, the sauce not too sweet and it's perfectly balanced with the sour chili sauce and mustard greens on the table. For 80 Bt you get a large portion. I really don't understand how anyone can complain about the price or the quality of this place.

There was no hype visible when I visited, but it would certainly deserve it.
