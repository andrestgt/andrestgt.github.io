# Street food & basic eateries

Chiang Mai has some of the best street food in Thailand, from Anthony Bourdain's famous pork leg stall to proper Santitham Road local spots that put most tourist restaurants to shame.

---

## A.T. Thaifoods
*Chang Phueak, Santitham · Street Food*
One of Santitham Road's highly recommended local spots — a very good version of khao kha moo at 50–60 Bt a plate, with more generous portions than a typical night market stall.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/at-thaifoods/)

---

## Chang Phuak Market
*Si Phum · Night Market*
Small, mostly tourist-oriented night market — the khao kha moo is one of the best anywhere, the Esarn stall has great larb and mango salad, and the old ladies at the tom yum stall are a must.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/chang-phuak-market/)

---

## Chang Phueak Pork Leg Rice
*Chang Phueak · Street Food*
Anthony Bourdain's famous pork stew stall — juicy, tender meat, a sauce that is not too sweet, perfectly balanced with sour chilli and mustard greens. Probably the best pork stew I've tried. 80 Bt.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/chang-phueak-pork-leg-rice/)

---

## Guay Jub Chang Moi Tat Mai
*Chang Moi · Street Food*
Very popular old-school pork noodle soup place — the rolled noodles and crispy pork are nice, but the 30-minute wait even off-peak is hard to justify for what it is.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/guay-jub-chang-moi-tat-mai/)

---

## Krua Rim Khlong
*Chang Khlan · Street Food*
A small hole-in-the-wall by the Khlong Mae Kha canal — best for a light snack, a drink, and a rest while exploring the neighbourhood. Good selection of Thai salads at very low prices.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/krua-rim-khlong/)

---

## Mark Burger & Steak
*Chang Phueak · Street Food, Burgers*
As far as budget burgers go in Thailand, this one is legit — cheap, tasty, and does the job perfectly.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/mark-burger/)

---

## Next-Level Som Tum
*Chang Phueak, Santitham · Street Food*
One of Santitham Road's best local spots — properly spicy, straightforward Thai food at rock-bottom prices with no concessions to tourist palates.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/next-level-som-tum/)

---

## Roti Pa Dae
*Chang Moi · Street Food*
Very good rotis with perfect texture and the right balance in the filling, fried in coconut oil — cheap and tasty, well worth a stop if the queue isn't too long.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/roti-pa-dae/)

---

## Tacos Bell Street Cart
*Si Phum, Old Town · Street Food*
Not exactly Mexican, but surprisingly tasty drinking food at reasonable prices — guilty pleasure comfort food done well.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/chiang-mai/street-food/tacos-bell/)
