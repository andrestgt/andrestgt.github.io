# Cafés

Bangkok has an extraordinary café scene, with hundreds of quality roasters spread across the city, from atmospheric canal-side spots to polished Sukhumvit specialty roasters.

---

## Air Space
*Lumphini, Pathum Wan · Café*
A spacious, professionally run café with reliably good espresso and reasonable prices for the Ratchadamri area — competent rather than exciting.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/air-space/)

---

## AKART Bistro & Bar
*Sathon · Specialty Coffee*
Excellent cappuccino for under 100 Bt, a well-designed interior, and free drinking water — a very easy place to recommend.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/akart/)

---

## Anan Cafe
*Phaya Thai · Café*
Surprisingly good beans for 50 Bt a cappuccino, though the preparation gives away that you're not paying more — slightly weak foam, served a touch too hot.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/anan-cafe/)

---

## Art Cafe & Bistro
*Phra Nakhon · Café*
A cosy little gem in an area not exactly flush with quality cafés, serving strong, well-prepared cappuccino at prices that would be hard to argue with anywhere in Bangkok.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/art-cafe/)

---

## Artis Coffee Bangkok
*Khlong Toei · Specialty Coffee*
The flat white is very good and the veranda pleasant enough — assuming you can tolerate Bangkok heat and traffic noise — though 110 Bt is a lot to tolerate alongside either.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/artis-coffee/)

---

## Bake Brothers
*Khlong Toei Nuea · Café*
A reliable mall café option inside Terminal 21, with decent cappuccino at reasonable prices and none of the pretension you'd expect elsewhere.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/bake-brothers/)

---

## Black Canyon – Ratchadaphisek
*Din Daeng · Café*
A quiet, spacious, and comfortable branch that does exactly what you expect from the chain: decent coffee and one of the better café-chain pad thais in the city.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/black-canyon-ratchada/)

---

## Black Canyon – Robinson Bang Rak
*Bang Rak · Café*
The atmosphere is uninspiring, cinnamon arrives on your cappuccino uninvited, but the pad thai is genuinely good — which for a café chain is something.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/black-canyon-bang-rak/)

---

## Brainwake Café
*Watthana · Café*
A very pleasant café with good coffee, honest prices, and authentic noodle soups — the only caveat being the compulsory cinnamon on cappuccino, which you'll need to head off at the pass.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/brainwake/)

---

## bridge art space
*Sathon · Café*
Good beans, well-prepared espresso drinks, inexpensive prices, and a friendly atmosphere — just order a latte or flat white and leave the cinnamon situation to someone else.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/bridge-art-space/)

---

## Café Amazon
*Bang Rak · Café*
Even by the modest standards of the chain, this branch disappoints: cramped, mediocre coffee, and hot drinks served in disposable cups even when consumed inside.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/cafe-amazon/)

---

## Chan Khanom Wan
*Samphanthawong, Chinatown · Café*
A beautifully decorated old-fashioned dessert café opposite the Chinatown gate that manages to serve a genuinely smooth 69 Bt cappuccino in a neighbourhood where most cafés with seating seem to regard Instagram as their primary product.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/chan-khanom-wan/)

---

## Coffee Beans By Dao – Central Embassy
*Pathum Wan · Café*
Luxuriously comfortable armchairs and a beautiful interior make this an attractive place to sit — the coffee is good if not quite worth the 100+ Bt, and the bistro menu follows the same pattern.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/coffee-beans-by-dao/)

---

## THE COFFEE CLUB – Mode Sathorn
*Bang Rak · Café*
One of the few Bangkok cafés that opens early and consistently delivers very good coffee — a flat white north of 130 Bt is the price you pay, and there are no power sockets to soften the blow.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/coffee-club-sathorn/)

---

## CPS Coffee
*Khlong Toei Nuea, Watthana · Café*
Very good coffee and a welcome specialist option at Terminal 21, though 120 Bt for a cappuccino in a setting without table service or comfortable seating is asking a lot, even by mall standards.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/cps-coffee/)

---

## D'ARK
*Watthana · Specialty Coffee*
The 115+ Bt cappuccino is genuinely the best in the city — smooth yet strong, in one of Bangkok's nicest café spaces — which is about as close to value for money as expensive gets.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/dark/)

---

## Dean & Deluca
*Bang Rak · Café & Deli*
Good coffee, decent cheesecake, and fancy surroundings at a price — the Cube branch upstairs is probably the best of them, with especially comfortable seating.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/dean-and-deluca/)

---

## ES/PE/RO
*Sathon · Specialty Coffee*
A cute little coffee shop opposite the Goethe-Institut where a very good flat white costs 70 Bt — extremely fair pricing for this neighbourhood, with no further justification needed.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/espero/)

---

## Happy Espresso
*Pom Prap · Café*
Not especially comfortable, but the air conditioning is fierce, the wifi fast, and the milk coffees made with premium beans are genuinely excellent at a very fair price.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/happy-espresso/)

---

## haus89 Samyan
*Bang Rak · Specialty Coffee*
A chic, modern coffee shop with well-prepared cappuccinos and good prices — slightly let down by the default plastic-cup service, though proper mugs are available on request.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/haus89/)

---

## H-Roasters
*Samphanthawong, Chinatown · Café*
An elegant, comfortable oasis in the middle of Chinatown, serving very good coffee at lower prices than you'd expect — a latte at 85 Bt is genuinely welcome here.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/h-roaster/)

---

## Jaywalk Cafe
*Phra Nakhon · Café*
Tiny and bare-bones — no sofa, no bathroom — but the smooth, well-prepared 70 Bt cappuccino makes it the best coffee option in an area that could use a few more of them.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/jaywalk/)

---

## KAFI Sathon
*Khlong San · Café*
A nicely decorated café with well-prepared flat whites at a very honest 60 Bt — straightforward and good, without pretending to be anything more.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/kafi-sathon/)

---

## Kaze Coffee
*Bang Rak · Café*
One of Bangkok's many small neighbourhood coffee shops, distinguished by a well-prepared 85 Bt latte — modest perhaps, but that's the point.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/kaze-coffee/)

---

## Mezzo
*Din Daeng · Café*
Essentially a local Starbucks imitation, which means the coffee is perfectly acceptable, the seats are comfortable, and a cappuccino is 70 Bt — ambition is not the selling point here.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/mezzo/)

---

## Mitr Roast x Brewsters
*Phaya Thai · Specialty Coffee*
Minimalist, seating-scarce, and excellent — an 80 Bt flat white of this quality is what good value actually looks like.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/mitr-roast/)

---

## Mother Roaster Talat Noi
*Samphanthawong, Talat Noi · Café*
The junk-garage entrance and selfie-crowded atmosphere are part of the deal in this very popular Talat Noi spot — the coffee is well made and the photographs will be fine, though 100 Bt for a latte is steep.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/mother-roaster/)

---

## Ninive – Wood-Fired Pizza
*Phra Khanong · Pizza, Café*
The best pizza option in the neighbourhood — fresh from a wood-fired oven, reasonably priced, and best consumed with an ice-cold Beer Lao.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/ninive/)

---

## NOB café
*Bang Rak · Specialty Coffee*
Friendly atmosphere, fair prices, and a choice of roasts, though the medium roast cappuccino leans bitter — the lighter option may be the better bet.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/nob-cafe/)

---

## (Not Just) Another Cup
*Bang Rak · Specialty Coffee*
The name practically writes the review: a nice cup and a nice space, but 100+ Bt for something that doesn't quite transcend the description "nice" is a stretch.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/not-just-another-cup/)

---

## Petit Peyton Travelogue Cafe
*Phra Nakhon · Café*
Bookshelves, old armchairs, and a genuinely cosy vintage atmosphere make this the nicest café in the area — well-prepared coffee at moderate prices seals the deal.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/petit-peyton/)

---

## Pobnar Specialty Coffee & Roastery
*Bang Rak · Specialty Coffee*
Hidden at the end of a narrow cul-de-sac in a Silom side street, it rewards the effort: one of Bangkok's best flat whites at under 100 Bt, in an aggressively hipster setup that the coffee justifies.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/pobnar/)

---

## Richdee's Eatery and Cafe
*Phra Nakhon · Café*
A pleasant canal-side stop in Banglamphu with good coffee at a very reasonable 60 Bt a latte — nothing exceptional, but the setting by the water elevates it above the ordinary.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/richdees/)

---

## Sané Phuthong
*Pom Prap · Café*
Possibly the most beautiful café in Bangkok, with a teak veranda, canal views, and Golden Mount temple in the frame — the 80 Bt latte being genuinely good makes it almost unfair to the competition.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/sane-phuthong/)

---

## Segafredo Zanetti Espresso – Rama 9
*Huai Khwang · Café*
One of the better Segafredo outlets — smooth, expertly prepared coffee under 100 Bt, no cinnamon, and comfy seats. A reliable Bangkok favourite.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/segafredo-rama-9/)

---

## Southern Coffee by Pakin
*Pathum Wan · Café*
Very comfy chairs, but horrible coffee served way too hot and overpriced. Avoid.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/southern-coffee/)

---

## Time's Coffee
*Bang Rak · Specialty Coffee*
A tiny shop with just a few tables — not for lingering, but the espresso drinks are excellent at 70 Bt. Great value.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/times-coffee/)

---

## Uni Coffee
*Bangkok · Café*
Paper cups only, but they make sense here — there's a terrace with sweeping views over Bangkok where you can enjoy a decent 90 Bt cappuccino.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/uni-coffee/)

---

## Vespa Cafe Coffee Roaster
*Phra Nakhon · Specialty Coffee*
A quirky soi café with very good espresso drinks at just 50 Bt — always order without cinnamon.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/cafes/vespa-cafe/)
