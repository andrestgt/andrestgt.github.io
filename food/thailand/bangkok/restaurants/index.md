# Restaurants

Bangkok has one of the greatest and most varied restaurant scenes in the world, from cheap Michelin-starred noodle shops to extraordinary fine dining at remarkably accessible prices.

---

## Al-Hussain Restaurant
*Khlong Toei Nuea, Watthana · Indian, Middle Eastern*
Authentic Muslim Indian and Middle Eastern food in large, sharing-designed portions — the brain masala alone is worth the trip.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/al-hussain/)

---

## Apinara
*Pathum Wan · Thai*
A genuinely upmarket Thai restaurant at Groove with stylish interiors and delicious food — the appetiser platter in particular — at prices that feel fair for the level of quality on the plate.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/apinara/)

---

## AV Izakaya Saphan Khwai
*Phaya Thai · Japanese, Izakaya*
Not your standard izakaya — the adult theme is unmistakable and the complimentary cake for female guests is exactly what you're imagining — but the skewers, the yukke, and the beer range prove there's genuine substance behind the spectacle.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/av-izakaya/)

---

## BADMOTEL
*Thong Lo, Watthana · Thai, Bar*
Thonglor prices apply, but happy hour until 8 pm softens the bill considerably, and both the Thai food and drinks — particularly the pad thai with three enormous prawns — are very good.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/badmotel/)

---

## Capri
*Wat Phraya Krai, Bang Kho Laem · Italian*
It looks like a tourist trap and isn't — especially on weekday two-for-one pizza nights, when this becomes one of the better-value Italian spots in Bangkok.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/capri/)

---

## Din Tai Fung Central Embassy
*Pathum Wan · Taiwanese, Dumplings*
The dumplings are reliably excellent, as expected — though Bangkok pricing runs noticeably higher than other branches in the region, and a full meal will cost you for the privilege.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/din-tai-fung/)

---

## Duklong Cafe and Eat
*Phra Nakhon · Thai*
Friendly service, a lovely khlong view, and fresh, tasty Thai cooking at very affordable prices — one of the nicest restaurants in the area for both atmosphere and value.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/duklong/)

---

## Fallabella River Front
*Khlong San · International*
The food and drinks are four-star territory, but the Chao Phraya view from ICONSIAM is five stars and basically the whole argument — the beef tongue won't disappoint either.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/fallabella/)

---

## G's
*Bang Rak · German*
A superb German Kneipe in an unlikely location, with a staggering beer selection including Bamberg smoked beer by the bottle, proper Flammkuchen, and prices that are entirely reasonable once you understand what you're getting.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/gs/)

---

## Gastro Kafe
*Phra Nakhon · Thai*
On Rambuttri there's nothing that stands out about it — except the 89 Bt big bottles of San Miguel and prices that haven't moved since 2019, which in a prime people-watching spot is something to celebrate.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/gastro-kafe/)

---

## Have a Zeed by Steak Lao
*Khlong Toei Nuea, Watthana · Isaan*
A comfortable, slightly upmarket mall option serving reliable Isaan food at reasonable prices — exactly what it is, done well.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/have-a-zeed/)

---

## Hemlock
*Phra Nakhon · Thai*
One of the best places for traditional Thai food in the Khao San area and has been for years — the atmosphere is genuinely good, and the prices remain very reasonable for it.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/hemlock/)

---

## Hiro Omakase
*Pathum Wan · Japanese, Omakase*
The negative reviews are undeserved: fresh, quality fish, perfect foie gras, and a gourmet experience for around 700 Bt per person — a difficult combination to fault.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/hiro-omakase/)

---

## Hong Bao Central Embassy
*Pathum Wan · Chinese, Dim Sum*
Extensive menu, consistently high-quality dim sum, and just a small step below Crystal Jade in both quality and pricing — which, to be clear, is a compliment.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/hong-bao/)

---

## HongKong Dimsum and Noodle
*Phra Nakhon · Chinese, Dim Sum*
A convenient modern noodle and dim sum spot on Phra Sumen Road, unfortunately let down by average quality and prices that don't justify the shortfall.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/hongkong-dimsum/)

---

## Kabocha Sushi at the Street
*Din Daeng · Japanese, Fusion*
A gourmet Japanese fusion experience — the foie gras unagi don alone earns its place on any personal highlights list — at prices so low for the quality that it's genuinely surprising this place isn't more famous.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/kabocha-sushi/)

---

## Krua Apsorn
*Phra Nakhon · Thai*
Canteen atmosphere, no frills, immensely popular, and rightfully so — the large Thai menu is very good even if everything skews sweet in the Bangkok fashion.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/krua-apsorn/)

---

## KubKao' KubPla Emquartier
*Watthana · Thai*
Secure a window seat in the Helix and this becomes a very attractive prospect — tasty, well-presented Thai dishes including some less common options, at prices that are fair for the setting.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/kubkao-kubpla/)

---

## Le Rêve Garden & Eatery
*Yan Nawa · Thai, International*
An off-the-beaten-track gem with a lovely garden, good service, and very tasty curries at reasonable prices — worth seeking out if you happen to be in this quiet part of town.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/le-reve/)

---

## Man Fu Yuan
*Watthana · Chinese, Dim Sum*
Very high-quality dim sum inside EmQuartier's Helix at prices that reflect the address — around 100–150 Bt a basket — though the quality and atmosphere comfortably justify both.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/man-fu-yuan/)

---

## Northeast
*Bang Rak · Isaan*
Very good Northeastern food from a busy open sidewalk kitchen, served in a blessedly air-conditioned dining room at prices that are genuinely reasonable for Sathorn.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/northeast/)

---

## Olive Kitchen – Khaosan
*Phra Nakhon · Italian, Pizza*
A wood-fired Neapolitan pizza spot with Thai craft beer that has no business being this good in the middle of the backpacker strip — worth the splurge over the cheaper options nearby.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/olive-kitchen/)

---

## Popiang
*Phra Nakhon · Thai*
Probably the best traveller café on Rambuttri — friendly service, consistently good food, guitar in the background, and a loyal following that has kept it going for a reason.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/popiang/)

---

## Praram 9 Kaiyang
*Pathum Wan · Thai, Grilled Chicken*
A good version of the grilled chicken, papaya salad, and sticky rice combination — Bangkok-sweet rather than punchy, but the sweeping views over CentralWorld and Pratunam make it worth the visit regardless.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/praram-9-kaiyang/)

---

## Reflections
*Pathum Wan · Thai Fusion*
Only average by Bangkok's exacting standards, but genuinely good value for Ratchadamri — duck larb and squid ink pasta with scallops are among the highlights.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/reflections/)

---

## Shoshana
*Phra Nakhon · Middle Eastern*
Big plates of tasty Middle Eastern comfort food at a reasonable price — been here for years and still a great place for a falafel fix. Come hungry.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/shoshana/)

---

## Sushi Den @ CentralWorld
*Pathum Wan · Japanese, Sushi*
Very good fresh sashimi at reasonable prices — the unagi roll is okay and the salmon head is good, though some dishes disappoint.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/sushi-den-ctw/)

---

## Sushi Den – Siam Paragon
*Pathum Wan · Japanese, Sushi*
Avoid this branch — smaller portions than CentralWorld, and the nigiri falls apart on touch.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/sushi-den-paragon/)

---

## SUSHIRO ICONSIAM
*Khlong San · Japanese, Sushi*
Fresh sushi at 40–80 Bt per plate of two nigiri — outstanding value, fully automated ordering, and now extremely popular. Expect a wait.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/sushiro/)

---

## Thipsamai Padthai Pratoopee
*Phra Nakhon · Thai, Pad Thai*
Bangkok's most famous pad thai — worth trying once, but not worth queuing more than two minutes. Nice, slightly sweet, with a couple of fresh king prawns.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/thipsamai/)

---

## Tim Ho Wan
*Pathum Wan · Chinese, Dim Sum*
Great quality dim sum, more expensive than Hong Kong but still worth a visit — the famous pork buns taste slightly sweeter here than the original.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/tim-ho-wan/)

---

## Wine Connection Rain Hill
*Watthana · Wine Bar, Deli*
The best place for oenophiles in the entire country — even the cheaper 500–600 Bt bottles are perfectly decent, and the food quality is good.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/wine-connection/)

---

## Wine I Love You
*Watthana · Wine Bar, International*
Generic bistro food at Bangkok standards, but good wine at reasonable prices and authentically spicy Thai dishes. The foie gras was well cooked but small.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/wine-i-love-you/)

---

## Yong He Dou Jiang
*Yan Nawa · Taiwanese, Dumplings*
A great little Taiwanese dumpling place — a couple of orders feeds an entire family, so don't come alone. Delicious.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/restaurants/yong-he-dou-jiang/)

---

**Price level note:**
Price levels refer to the approximate cost for **two people** ordering an average meal **without alcoholic drinks**.
