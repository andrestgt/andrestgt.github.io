I came across The Junction near the pier after spotting a sign advertising craft beer and Neapolitan pizza, which was more than enough to make me stop.

They were unfortunately out of craft beer when I visited, apparently after a party the night before, but the wood-fired pizzas more than made up for it. I went for the larb moo pizza, my usual go-to in Thailand, and it was spicy, delicious, and genuinely very satisfying.

At under 300 baht, prices are very reasonable, especially for proper wood-fired pizza, and it makes a very worthwhile pit stop if you are exploring Bang Kachao by bicycle.
