One of my favourite breakfasts in the world. Gai yang, or grilled chicken with som tam and sticky rice. The twist with this Isaan specialty is that the chicken is flattened and marinated before it's grilled, this way the normally boring breast meat gets a lot more juicy and interesting. This basic hole-in-the-wall does a nice Bangkok style version of gai yang, less spicy and a bit sweeter than what you'd get in the North East of Thailand, but it doesn't make it worse, in fact it's probably nicer for most palates. A whole chicken goes for only 130 Bt which is a great price for Bangkok. Highly recommended.
