# Street food & basic eateries

Bangkok has one of the great street food cultures in the world, from 25 Bt charcoal-grilled ho mok in Chinatown to Michelin-recommended noodle shops and legendary canal-side hole-in-the-wall spots.

---

## Baan Glang Soi
*Bang Rak · Thai, Noodles*
An inexpensive, neat little place in a quiet Bang Rak side street — doubles as a 50 Bt noodle soup kitchen in the mornings.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/baan-glang-soi/)

---

## Burn Baby Burn
*Din Daeng · Craft Beer, Burgers*
Cool place in Rot Fai market with good craft beer — the burgers are photogenic but not worth the price tag.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/burn-baby-burn/)

---

## Hero Moo Plara
*Phra Nakhon · Thai, Grilled Meats*
Cheap platters of charcoal grilled meats and sausages with delicious dipping sauces, plus Leo from the tap. Always open. Exactly what you want.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/hero-moo-plara/)

---

## Jae Hong Stir Fried Chicken Noodle
*Pom Prap · Street Food*
Deep inside a small alley market — deservedly popular for its fried chicken and char kway teow with fresh king prawn from the charcoal oven. Tasty and inexpensive.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/jae-hong/)

---

## Jae Sri Ho Mok Sampheng
*Samphanthawong · Street Food*
Charcoal grilled ho mok for just 25 Bt each — no English spoken, just point. One of Bangkok's great cheap eats.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/jae-sri-ho-mok/)

---

## Jodd Fairs Night Market
*Huai Khwang · Night Market*
Visually spectacular and good for first-time visitors — but the food is overpriced and overhyped, and the Chinese tour groups are relentless.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/jodd-fairs/)

---

## Kai Yang Wichian Buri
*Din Daeng · Thai, Grilled Chicken*
Bangkok-style gai yang, flattened and marinated before grilling — juicier and more interesting than most versions. A whole chicken for 130 Bt. One of the best breakfasts in the world.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/kai-yang-wichian-buri/)

---

## Khanom Jeep Tom Hia Sek
*Bangkok Yai · Street Food, Dumplings*
Really delicious dumplings — thin dough, tasty filling, 100 Bt for a box of 10. Found at Terminal 21 among other locations.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/khanom-jeep/)

---

## Krua Porn La Mai
*Samphanthawong · Street Food, Seafood*
Very good street food despite the queues and YouTubers — fresh seafood, 500 Bt feeds four people, hot plates, nice tom yum. A second visit revealed inconsistency, hence the revised rating.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/krua-porn-la-mai/)

---

## Laab Ubon Kontrakran
*Yan Nawa, Sathon · Isaan*
An inexpensive, cheerful Isaan open-air spot hidden in pricey Sathorn — good gai yang, larb ped, and som tum at 80–100 Bt a dish.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/laab-ubon/)

---

## Madamzabb
*Don Mueang · Thai*
A tidy hole-in-the-wall near Don Mueang airport with a short menu of absolutely delicious Thai standards at rock-bottom prices. Try the crab fried rice.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/madamzabb/)

---

## Pan Kham Hom – The Street Ratchada
*Din Daeng · Food Court*
A pleasant, rarely crowded food court with good quality food and reasonable prices — the Khao Soi stall (T12) is the highlight.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/pan-kham-hom/)

---

## Rung Rueang Pork Tung Noodle
*Khlong Toei · Street Food, Noodles*
A basic but popular noodle place on Sukhumvit 26 — good for a quick bowl of dry tom yum with fish crackers and cheap for the area.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/rung-rueang/)

---

## Sawang Noodle
*Bang Rak · Noodles*
A Michelin Bib Gourmand that looks exactly like what it is — a humble noodle shop with tasty wonton and roasted pork at 100 Bt a bowl. Don't go out of your way, but visit if you're nearby.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/sawang-noodle/)

---

## SOOKSIAM
*Khlong San · Street Food Court*
A stunning indoor street food court inside ICONSIAM — all your favourite Thai street food at surprisingly low prices in luxurious surroundings. A must-visit for any Bangkok trip.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/sooksiam/)

---

## Sung Phochana
*Phra Nakhon · Noodles*
Excellent beef noodle soup in a lovely canal-side setting — a generous bowl with different types of meat for just 70 Bt.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/sung-phochana/)

---

## Wan Dee
*Yan Nawa, Sathon · Noodles*
Very good, flavourful beef noodle soup with tender meat for just 50 Bt — no English menu but some English spoken.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/wan-dee/)

---

## Yentafo Phua Ki
*Phra Nakhon · Thai-Chinese, Noodles*
A good old-fashioned Thai-Chinese hole in the wall — delicious shrimp wontons and generous prawn portions. Inexpensive, a good choice on Phra Athit.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/yentafo-phua-ki/)

---

## Yok Saeng Beef Noodle
*Pom Prap · Noodles*
Old-school beef noodle shop with clear broth and nicely marinated meat for 70 Bt — English menu available, not much English spoken.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/yok-saeng-beef-noodle/)

---

## Zabb One Ratchada
*Huai Khwang · Isaan*
Very popular Isaan spot right at the MRT — Bangkok-style, so less spicy and a bit sweeter, but good for sampling many dishes. The kai yang is the star.
→ [Read the full review](/food/thailand/bangkok/street-food/zabb-one/)
