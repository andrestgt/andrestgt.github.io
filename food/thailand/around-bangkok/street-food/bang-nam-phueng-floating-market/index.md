Despite the name, this is not really a floating market, but more of a typical Thai weekend market set along the canals, with food stalls, small vendors, and plenty to eat. It only takes place on Saturdays and Sundays and seems to have grown from a bit of an insider tip into something quite popular. Traffic around the area can get heavy, as most people arrive by car. It's much better to come by bicycle. Riding through the surrounding green countryside is a real highlight and really what makes the experience. The market itself is very nice, but nothing out of the ordinary. Prices are low, the food is good, and overall it's a great way to spend a few hours out in Samut Prakan.
