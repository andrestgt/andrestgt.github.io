Probably the best of the Thai-Indian bunch in Ao Nang. Anyone who's wandered about Ao Nang's main road will soon notice that the culinary scene in town is dominated by Indians. Restaurants abound, all offering pretty much the same mix of Indian and Thai food with some pizza and pasta thrown in for tourists that can't eat Asian food. We tried quite a few of them — Namasta, Taj Palace — and they really feel pretty similar, in price and drab ambiance, as well as the food which is okay, but nothing special. They all offer very good low season discounts on their otherwise pretty high prices. But it was Tandoori Nights which stood out. Not only is their decor nicer than others, they also had hands down the best food, Thai as well as Indian. The Chicken Tikka Masala was truly sublime, one of the best I ever had, and their breads are delicious too. The Thai Massaman Curry was lovely too, and the happy hour cocktails were not only tasty but a real bargain by Thai standards. As most places around town they offer 20–25% low season discount on all food, and despite the high quality food, their regular prices are the same as elsewhere — so this is clearly the place of choice when it comes to Indian-Thai in Ao Nang. Recommended!
