I have been frequenting Casablanca for more than a decade now and never had a bad meal. The Austrian owner is a proper restaurateur and knows his business.
It's hard to find consistently good food in the provinces in the Philippines and Casablanca is a heaven sent.
If this was in Bangkok or Singapore it wouldn't be a five star place, for sure, this is simple food, not some creative fine dining, but in the Visayas you won't find anything better than this.
Casablanca is in a prime location on Rizal Blvd and on nice days you can enjoy the view over the deep blue ocean and Siquijor. Since traffic in Dumaguete is getting worse every year, however, the air conditioned dining room is a good alternative.
Guenther, the proprietor, is also serious about wines, and he offers a decent collection of reasonably priced, good value wines to accompany his food.
Casablanca serves very eclectic international fare, from seared tuna to hearty Central European meat platters. There's also a small menu of Filipino favourites, which are as good as any you can find in the region's local restaurants.
Their lunch deals are incredible. 270 Php will buy you a soup, a salad, a beer and a generous main course.
One of the reasons I'm writing this review is to express my complete incredulity about some people on Google reviews complaining that it is too expensive.
It really isn't! It's actually incredibly good value for what you get.
I'd even go as far as to say, for the same amount of meat they serve here, you'd pay more in some basic local carinderia, and the taste won't be half as good.
The service tends to be pretty bad, that's true, especially when the owner is out of town, which can be quite frustrating to be honest. But that's the same everywhere in the country and the quality of the food that comes out of the kitchen has never disappointed.
Casablanca is quite simply a must visit for everyone who likes wine and good food when in the Visayas.
