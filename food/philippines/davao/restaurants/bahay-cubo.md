Samal, Davao del Norte, Philippines

Samal island is not a place for foodies, but Alphonse and Jaqueline's Bahay Kubo is a hidden gem and a must for everyone visiting. Open Friday to Sunday only, it is a labour of love with no fixed menu â€” Alphonse offers whatever is available, usually a good selection of meat and fish dishes, soup, and dessert. The tuna steak was perfectly cooked and truly divine, the roast beef melted in your mouth, and the beef meatballs with fried potatoes were some of the best I have had in Asia. They also boast a nice variety of good-value wines, mainly from Chile, perfectly chilled. Head to Fernandez Beach and follow the signs.
