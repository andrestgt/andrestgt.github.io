Frankly, this is a terrible restaurant, it's cramped and designed like a cafeteria and the patrons seem to consist exclusively of rude, arrogant old family types who have been running and ruining this country for centuries.
However, there is a very good reason to come here, the Spanish-Filipino food they serve here is quite delicious and the waitresses, while not very competent, try their best to make you feel comfortable.
Out of their extensive menu featuring most classics and some innovations, we chose the delicious calamares rellenos en su tinta, the callos tripe stew and the braised tongue lengua estofado.
Most dishes are under 300 Php, portions are big and the food is really tasty.
They don't seem to serve wine there, but i didn't ask for corkage fees, so if you come here for dinner maybe it's a good idea to call ahead and bring your own.
