When I first visited El Nido in the late 00s, the Art Café had some unique selling points — exotic things like cappuccino and WiFi. Back then, it was pricier than its competitors but offered a level of quality (including boat tours) that was a notch above the rest.
Nowadays, it feels like a relic — a dinosaur, its sheer size making it stand out in a town where most places are much smaller. It’s no longer the focal point for travelers, but it still gets things right. The décor is nicer than it used to be, the prices are now pretty much on par with the competition, and the second-floor terrace offers pleasant views. In a town not exactly known for exceptional food, the meals here are absolutely fine, too.
