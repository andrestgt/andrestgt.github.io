Las Flores is a good looking, modern restaurant with good service and a classic (one might say unadventurous) menu which includes most of our favourite small plates.
Among the five dishes that we ordered, none stood out and none fell flat, it was all tasty, well prepared and nice to look at.
Scallop ceviche with jamón, tuna escabeche (big portion!), pulpo gallega (tender)...all very nice. The beef with porcini and pine nuts could have done with more mushrooms, but it was good nevertheless.
Their mango tatin is recommended for dessert, an easy winner in the country with the best mangoes in the world.
With tapas prices around 400 Php, Las flores is pricey even by BGC standards but they have some good lunch deals, as well as a very reasonably priced and extensive wine list which almost makes up for it.
