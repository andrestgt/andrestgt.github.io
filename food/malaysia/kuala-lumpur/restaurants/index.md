# Restaurants

---

## Din Tai Fung – Pavilion KL
*Bukit Bintang, KL · Chinese, Dim Sum*
Probably the best Din Tai Fung anywhere — the truffle xiao long bao and spicy wontons are absolutely superb.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/din-tai-fung-pavilion-kl/)

---

## Halab KL Arabic Restaurant
*Bukit Bintang, KL · Lebanese*
One of Bukit Bintang's best Middle Eastern restaurants — grown from a shawarma takeaway into a polished Lebanese institution at reasonable prices.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/halab-kl/)

---

## Ben's Independent Grocer
*Publika, KL · Specialty Foods*
One of the best supermarkets in the region — excellent imported products, wines, oysters and a proper espresso bar.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/bens-independent-grocer/)

---

## Peranakan Place Signature
*NU Sentral, KL · Nyonya*
Popular Nyonya chain with bold, pungent flavours that haven't been toned down — the rendang with pandan rice is particularly recommended.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/peranakan-place-signature/)

---

## Plan B – Publika
*Publika, KL · Fusion*
Creative and very well executed interpretations of nasi lemak and unagi don — genuinely excellent food, if rather expensive.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/plan-b-publika/)

---

## Taj Garden
*Brickfields, KL · Indian*
Clean mid-range Indian restaurant in Little India with authentic flavours, excellent mango lassis, and fair prices.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/taj-garden/)

---

## TG's Bistro
*Bukit Bintang, KL · Indian, Malay*
Very useful in Bukit Bintang for inexpensive Indian-Malay food — consistently good tandoor dishes, breads, and cheap nasi kandar.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/tgs-bistro/)

---

## Grandmama's
*Pavilion KL, Bukit Bintang · Malay*
Very good Malay food that genuinely tastes close to proper street versions, served in air-conditioned mall comfort.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/grandmamas/)

---

## Dolly Dim Sum
*NU Sentral, KL · Chinese, Dim Sum*
Reliable dim sum franchise serving genuinely tasty dumplings at sensible mid-range prices — a solid choice around NU Sentral.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/dolly-dim-sum/)

---

## Dongbai
*KL · Chinese, Northeastern*
Cute family-run restaurant with handmade noodles, dumplings and fiery Manchurian-style meat dishes.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/dongbai/)

---

## MTR – KL Sentral
*Brickfields, KL · South Indian*
Good basics done properly — vadas and idlis for breakfast, with the mini thali only from 11:30; pricier than the Bangalore original.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/mtr-kl-sentral/)

---

## Michelangelo's Ristorante Italiano
*Pavilion KL · Italian*
Pleasant people-watching with decent house wines during happy hour — the Italian food is expensive but enjoyable alongside a bottle of wine.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/michelangelos/)

---

## Sushi Tei
*Pavilion KL, Bukit Bintang · Japanese*
A former favourite that has gradually declined — smaller portions and more compromises compared to earlier years.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/sushi-tei/)

---

## Luk Yu Tea House
*The Starhill, Bukit Bintang · Chinese, Dim Sum*
High-quality dim sum in a wonderfully elegant atmosphere — the beverage prices, however, are bordering on absurd.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/luk-yu-tea-house/)

---

## Ying Ker Lou Hakka Cuisine
*The Curve, Petaling Jaya · Chinese, Dim Sum*
Very solid dim sum around The Curve area — steamed dumplings noticeably stronger than the fried items.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/ying-ker-lou-hakka-cuisine/)

---

## Canton-i Restaurant
*The Gardens Mall, Mid Valley · Chinese, Dim Sum*
Decent Cantonese dim sum when stuck in Mid Valley — quality is acceptable though prices are slightly higher than they should be.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/canton-i-restaurant/)

---

## Nyonya Colors – Suria KLCC
*KLCC · Malay, Nyonya*
Decent enough and reasonably priced for the location — proper Malay food options are surprisingly limited in KLCC.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/nyonya-colors-suria-klcc/)

---

## Nyonya Tingkat – NU Sentral
*NU Sentral · Nyonya*
Genuinely good Nyonya flavours let down by stingy portions of meat and seafood considering the prices.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/nyonya-tingkat-nu-sentral/)

---

## ANJAPPAR Chettinad Indian Restaurant
*Brickfields · Indian*
Authentic South Indian food at average prices and quality — perfectly alright but never stands out in a competitive field.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/anjappar-chettinad/)

---

## Ichiban Boshi
*Publika · Japanese*
Works well enough as a reasonably priced fallback in Publika — quality slightly below Sushi Tei or Zanmai but still decent.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/ichiban-boshi/)

---

## Fuel Shack Signature
*Low Yat Plaza · Burgers, Malaysian*
Disappointing overall — soggy bun and flavourless nasi lemak in a city with so much outstanding food.
→ [Read the full review](/food/malaysia/kuala-lumpur/restaurants/fuel-shack-signature/)
