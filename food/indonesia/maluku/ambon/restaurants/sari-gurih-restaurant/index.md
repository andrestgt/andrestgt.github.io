I visited Sari Gurih twice in Ambon, once at the beach location and once at the branch in the city. The setting here is obviously less appealing. Instead of sitting above the water with views over the bay, the restaurant itself feels rather institutional, though the service was efficient and the menu is the same.

I ordered the ikan kuah kuning again, the turmeric-based fish soup that is one of the local specialties. The broth was still excellent, fragrant and well balanced, but the fish was noticeably different from what I had at the beach branch. Instead of the firm white grouper-like fish served there, the kitchen used a mix of different fish here, some pieces chewy and quite bony.

The seafood capcay was very good, cooked with plenty of crunchy vegetables. Prices remain reasonable, and if you are staying in the centre of Ambon it is still a convenient place for a solid meal, even if the beach branch is the more enjoyable setting.
