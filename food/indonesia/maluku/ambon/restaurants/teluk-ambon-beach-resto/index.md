We came here for a late lunch and had the place entirely to ourselves. It's a large open-air restaurant in a very pleasant seaside setting. The decoration is fairly simple, but the location right by the water is definitely attractive.

We ordered squid in butter sauce, broccoli with prawns, and fish bistik. I had expected something closer to a grilled fish steak, but it turned out to be more like an Indonesian-style version of fish and chips with sweet and sour sauce. Not exactly what I had in mind, but it was actually quite tasty.

Most mains were around 80K, which is very reasonable for a seaside restaurant. A good option for a relaxed lunch by the water.
