I had dinner at Nusantara only once, during my last days in Banda Neira. The place is popular with tourists and, importantly for Banda, it is usually open during the day.

We tried the fried aubergines with kenari sauce, ulang-ulang, and ayam bakar. The aubergines and the ulang-ulang were fine, though fairly standard. The ayam bakar was disappointing. Instead of proper grilled chicken meat, I was served a couple of small wings completely drowned in sweet kecap manis. That was not what I expected when ordering grilled chicken.

Prices are moderate and slightly lower than some other tourist-oriented places on the island. Service was friendly.

Overall, Nusantara is a decent option when other places are closed, but the cooking was the least memorable of the meals I had in Banda Neira.
