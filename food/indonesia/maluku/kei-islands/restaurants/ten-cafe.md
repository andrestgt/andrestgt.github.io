Ten Café is the only proper restaurant around Ngurbloat, and that is why most tourists end up eating here. The setting is excellent, with direct views over the wide stretch of white sand.

All the food is freshly prepared, so it takes a while to arrive, but it is cooked with care. I really liked the black pepper squid and prawns, as well as their version of sea grape ceviche, which you find all over the island, but this was my favourite.

The grilled seafood is not quite as good as in the more local places around Langgur, and prices are roughly 30% higher. Still, for anyone staying around Ngurbloat, this is a godsend. Expect to pay around 100 to 150K per person including drinks.
