Zum Becher has apparently been around for a long time, even if I have to admit I'd never heard of it until recently. As a traditional Swabian restaurant in the centre of Stuttgart, my first visit was a thoroughly positive experience.

On a Tuesday evening, the place was extremely busy, so booking ahead is strongly recommended. The menu focuses on the absolute classics of Swabian cooking, and that's exactly what they do very well here.

I ordered the Schwabenteller, one of the more expensive dishes on the menu, featuring Zwiebelrostbraten, Maultaschen, Käsespätzle and gravy. Everything was convincing: a clearly homemade, full-bodied gravy, a Zwiebelrostbraten cooked perfectly medium, bordering on medium-rare, alongside well-seasoned Maultaschen and creamy Käsespätzle.

Prices are perfectly reasonable for Stuttgart. Certainly not cheap, but entirely appropriate for the quality on offer.

One possible criticism is that the portions are not particularly generous. By Swabian standards, they're actually on the small side. Personally, though, I didn't mind that at all. You leave comfortably satisfied rather than completely stuffed.

Another plus is the wine list, which features a good selection of regional wines, including bottles from Strümpfelbach and Beutelsbach. Prices are fair and the quality is there to match.

Overall, a clear recommendation for anyone looking for refined, traditional Swabian cooking, very friendly service, and solid value for money.
