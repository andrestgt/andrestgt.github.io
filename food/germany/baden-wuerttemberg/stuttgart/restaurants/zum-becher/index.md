Zum Becher has apparently been around for a long time, even if I have to admit I'd never heard of it until recently. As a traditional Swabian restaurant in the centre of Stuttgart, my first visit was thoroughly positive. On a Tuesday evening it was extremely busy, so booking is strongly recommended. The menu sticks to the absolute classics of Swabian cooking — and that's exactly what they do very well here. I had the Schwabenteller, one of the more expensive dishes on the menu, with Zwiebelrostbraten, Maultaschen, Käsespätzle and roasting jus. Everything was genuinely convincing: a clearly homemade, full-bodied jus, a Zwiebelrostbraten cooked perfectly medium — almost medium rare — together with well-seasoned Maultaschen and creamy Käsespätzle. Pricing is absolutely within range for Stuttgart — certainly not cheap, but appropriate. One possible criticism: the portions aren't lavish. By Swabian standards rather small. I personally found that pleasant, though; you don't have to roll out of the place completely stuffed. For me the portion was just right. Pleasing too is the wine list: there's a good selection of regional wines on offer, including from Strümpfelbach and Beutelsbach. Prices are absolutely fair, quality likewise. Overall, a clear recommendation for a refined, traditional, gutbürgerlich restaurant with very good, friendly service and decent value.
