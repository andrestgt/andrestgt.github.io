From the outside this place doesn't look like much, and the décor is more reminiscent of a simple Asian takeaway. The menu, too, features the typical Germanised Asian dishes you find all over the place. But behind this unassuming façade hides a really good, authentic Sichuan restaurant.

Here you get the region's characteristically fiery, oily and incredibly flavour-intense dishes. The menu also includes more unusual specialities such as pork intestine with duck blood or lamb tripe, as well as down-to-earth dishes like spicy noodles with beef.

The prices are considerably higher than the simple setting would suggest, but the quality is definitely there.
