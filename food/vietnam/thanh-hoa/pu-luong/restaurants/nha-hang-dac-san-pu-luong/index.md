Pu Luong Auberge is probably the most spectacular restaurant in the province, with absolutely stunning views of the rice terraces and the surrounding mountain range. The setting alone is worth the visit.

It’s a large place with a quirky, eclectic interior and even an Italian pizza oven, although I only tried the northern Vietnamese hors d'oeuvre platter and some fried rice, as we were there for brunch and the pizza oven wasn’t operating yet.

The food is absolutely fine. It’s nothing particularly outstanding, but that’s not really the point. If you’re in the area, you simply have to stop here, even if it’s only for a drink. The views are amazing, and it’s a place you can’t afford to miss.
