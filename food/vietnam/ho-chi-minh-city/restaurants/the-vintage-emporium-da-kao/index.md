I'll have to agree with some reviewers who find this place nice but really overpriced. Now, I'm not one of those silly people who complain that the food here is too expensive for Vietnam, just because they can get some cheap noodles on the street. I know the real estate prices in Saigon, and how much imported ingredients cost, so charging "Western" prices is nothing to complain about. But charging high prices is only justified when you offer high quality, and that's where the Vintage Emporium disappointed a little. The cappuccino for 60k was very good, so no complaints here. However, the Moroccan poached eggs were mediocre. It's fine to charge 160k for 2 eggs, tomato and cheese Moroccan style, if they come in a sumptuous gravy of tomatoes, Harissa and Ras El Hanout, covered with a generous amount of real sheep milk feta, yes that would be a wonderful dish. But what I was served here were two eggs in a relatively bland tomato sauce with just a few tiny cubes of boring cow milk 'feta' on top. It wasn't bad, it's an alright meal, but just not good value and nothing making me crave for more.
