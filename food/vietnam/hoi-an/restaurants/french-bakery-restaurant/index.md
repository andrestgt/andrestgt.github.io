Despite the name, this is a simple family restaurant dishing out standard local favourites to a mostly Western clientele. They do adjust their food to be more suited to the Western palate, but that's not necessarily bad if it's done right, and while I'm still not sure about their egg dough Banh Xeo (literally pancakes) , I generally enjoyed the food.

The Ngheu hap (steamed mussels in lemongrass broth) were fresh and authentic, the Cha gio tom (prawn rolls) came with salad and herbs and the fries were crispy and home made. Prices are modest and suitable for the location and ambiance, about 100 k per pax for a full meal with drinks.
