Olivia has been around and busy for many years which means they must be doing something right.

It is indeed a charming, rustic restaurant, certainly one of the more attractive places to eat in Nha Trang's tourist area.

However, it's not a place for authentic Italian culinary sensations, i'd be very surprised if they ever had an Italian chef or manager here. The food they serve is tourist Italian for undemanding eaters that fancy pizza and pasta but have never heard of ossobucco or porchetta.

Their pizzas are so so, reasonably priced, and definitely not the worst in town. The spinach ravioli 'Boscaiola' were surprisingly nice, they seemed home-made and came with proper cream.

The beef carpaccio, however, was a disaster. It was killed by disgusting, cheap olive oil which overpowered all the flavours of the beef and even the parmesan.

I would avoid all dishes with olive oil dressing here, they use a really nasty one.

Also make sure you avoid their house wine which comes out of a box at Nha Trang room temperature (about 30 degrees).

On a positive note, the service here is good and friendly and the menu is still pre-Russian invasion, therefore very reasonably priced (although i'd be willing to pay a little more, so they can afford to use some decent olive oil).
