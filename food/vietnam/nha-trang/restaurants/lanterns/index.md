Lanterns has been Nha Trang's most popular tourist restaurant for many years now but i never really saw why, as i was slightly underwhelmed by my first visit, and especially disappointed by their signature claypot dish, which tasted sweet and boring with medium quality fish.

I remembered, however, that the other dishes i ordered were just fine, and decided to give it another try when i ran out of dining options.

And it turned out to be a pleasant surprise. First mention needs to go to the wine list which is very good, and very affordable by Nha Trang standards, with a perfectly drinkable Ardèchois as house wine for less than 300k a bottle.

All three dishes we ordered were very good. The Banh Xeo was just perfect, with very thin dough, a strong sauce and a good amount of seafood inside. Although for almost 100k the prawns could have been of the bigger variety.

The Bo La Lot was excellent, juicy with the right amount of well balanced spices.

The fried squid was fresh and tender, the side fries were not great though.

All in all a very good meal.

The place gets packed at night so for a pleasant quiet experience it's better to visit during the less busy hours, before noon, or in the afternoon for 20% discount on the bill.

Good service by Nha Trang standards.
