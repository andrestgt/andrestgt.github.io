We went to Skylight and its Chef's Club restaurant for a special Thai set menu, allegedly prepared by a Michelin starred chef.

We came early enough to enjoy the sunset from the rooftop, but annoying Chinese tourists sadly ruined the atmosphere. These are most likely hotel guests and not customers of Skylight, so I guess there's not much they can do about this annoyance.

The views, however, are gorgeous and worth a visit to Skylight alone. Best to avoid weekends I guess.

Now for the food. The dinner was priced at 600k ++ for a 5 course meal which is quite reasonable for such a location, but to be honest, the food served was pretty simple straightforward Thai food, authentically spicy, but nothing out of the ordinary.

It was generally tasty though and decent value.

They also offered a 300k ++ free flow of wine, supposedly from South Africa. But I could swear the white was ordinary Dalat plonk, completely undrinkable. The red, however, was a decent, drinkable non Dalat wine, possibly from South Africa, and chilled it went well with the spicy food. And our glasses were promptly refilled from a decanter.

So all in all, I'd say it's a must visit for the views, but there's still room for improvement in all other areas
