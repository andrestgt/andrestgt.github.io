This charming little alley bar opened just a few days ago and is a great addition to Nha Trang’s modest craft beer scene. We tried a sampler of four beers—two of which I really liked, while the other two, though not my favorites, were still cheap and refreshing. Even better are their beer cocktails, with big glasses going for under 100k.

The biggest surprise, though, is the food. Their Thai-style raw jellyfish and prawn salad (125k) is as good as anything you’d get in Bangkok, and their tapas sampler plate featured some perfectly marinated boiled beef—so this is a bar where you could come just for a good meal as well.

I first wrote these lines in May 2019, just a few months before Vietnam was closed off due to the pandemic and Jelly had just opened. It quickly became a meeting spot for stranded backpackers and has since turned into a full-throttle backpacker/expat haven, packed almost every night.

Now, in 2025, the beer cocktails are still 90k, and I still enjoy them. The food menu has remained largely the same, too. If you can actually find a seat and don’t mind loud music, it’s still an easy recommendation, although I certainly wouldn't describe it as charming, little bar anymore.
