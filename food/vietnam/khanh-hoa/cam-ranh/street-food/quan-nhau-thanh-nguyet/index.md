Accidentally finding a place like this is one of the reasons why travelling around Vietnam is still one of a foodie's life's greatest pleasures. This simple drinking place doesn't even look like a proper eatery from the outside. The location is as bad as it gets, along an industrial, noisy stretch of Highway 1 in the outskirts of non descript Cam Ranh City. We stopped only because they had a bia tuoi sign outside, it was a hot summer day and we were thirsty. Little did we know that we ended up having one of the best meals in a long time here. There's quite an extensive menu of really interesting dishes, not just the usual quan nhau (drinking place) standard fare, and everything is freshly prepared and of very good quality. Their specialties are 'one day sun dried grilled beef with chili ants' and the ridiculously delicious muc/tom mam nhi, essentially raw squid/prawns in a bowl of smooth highest quality fish sauce. Another great and rare dish is their oxtail stew with peanuts. Being close to the sea, it's also no wonder that their grilled fish dishes (ca mu or grouper is always a good choice) are fresh and tasty. All dishes cost less than 100k and a litre of Saigon Bia Hoi is 15k, great value for food of this quality.
