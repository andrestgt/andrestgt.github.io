There are several sidewalk bbq joints on this street, but Pho Co is by far the most popular. After trying quite a bit of their food, grilled dishes and others I know why. It's not only friendly, but really great quality. It starts with the fact that they serve a really nice buckwheat craft beer (50k) instead of just the usual cheap lagers. The dip that comes with the grilled meat dishes is delicious, a chili sauce with ginger and lime leaf, all home made. There's no industrial meat here either, it's all farmer's meat and hand made sausages, and they're damn tasty, the Hmong sausages (10k a piece) and the charcoal smoked buffalo sausages (50k a piece). They also serve the local specialty Chao Au Tau (35k), a chicken porridge with a special root that is potentially toxic if the cook doesn't know how to prepare it, but apparently it's very healthy when served in the proper way.
Mind, you might sit on tiny plastic chairs on the street and get your food served on plastic plates, but this is not cheap food. Even in the Highlands of Northern Vietnam free range meat and craft beers cost money, but the prices here are very reasonable. For 150-200k per person you can have a nice feast here, including drinks. Highly recommended
