I generally don't dislike little traveller restaurants with their watered down versions of local specialties, quite often indeed, you get perfectly tasty food for very reasonable prices, rarely great, but usually good. So I expected a bit more here than what I got, but then there wasn't much choice in these corona times, very few places were open for lunch. I also understand that not the whole menu is available with few customers around. But that doesn't explain the extremely poor quality of their Bun Cha Hanoi (supposedly their specialty, 30k). A good bun cha needs a balanced sauce (sweet, sour, salty) with high quality fish sauce and the flavour of the barbecue where the meatballs are grilled. Here the sauce was off balance with too much acid and most likely artificial fish sauce, plus the meat was fried not grilled and tasted just very porky. I really was awful.

The fried spring rolls (50k) were fine, but came with chili sauce from the bottle as the only dip, and without any salad, leaves and herbs for wrapping. Again, not good enough, even for a tourist place.

At least it was cheap
