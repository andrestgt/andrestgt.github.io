# Cafés

Good coffee is genuinely hard to find in Luang Prabang, most places over-roast and add cinnamon uninvited.

---

## Le Café Ban Vat Sene
*Sakkaline Rd · Café*
The best cappuccino in Luang Prabang, in a beautiful French colonial setting with excellent tartines, expensive by local standards but worth it.
→ [Read the full review](/food/laos/luang-prabang/cafes/le-cafe-ban-vat-sene/)

---

## Joma Bakery Café – Quiet Branch
*Luang Prabang · Café*
The quieter Joma branch outperforms the main one, ask them to skip the cinnamon and you'll get the best cappuccino in town.
→ [Read the full review](/food/laos/luang-prabang/cafes/joma-bakery-cafe-ii/)

---

## Le Banneton Café & French Bakery
*Sakkaline Rd · Bakery, Café*
Lovely French-style café with very decent croissants, the coffee disappoints, but the pastries alone make it worth a stop.
→ [Read the full review](/food/laos/luang-prabang/cafes/le-banneton-cafe/)

---

## Delicieux Café & Restaurant
*Luang Prabang · Café*
Pleasant air-conditioned café with decent coffee, slightly better and cheaper than Joma's main branch, though seating is uncomfortable.
→ [Read the full review](/food/laos/luang-prabang/cafes/delicieux-cafe/)

---

## Saffron Coffee
*Luang Prabang · Café*
Lovely Mekong views and comfortable armchairs undercut by bitter beans and unnecessary cinnamon.
→ [Read the full review](/food/laos/luang-prabang/cafes/saffron-coffee/)

---

## Joma Bakery Café – Chao Fa Ngum
*Chao Fa Ngum Rd · Café*
A beautiful café in an old colonial villa that used to be among the best in Laos; quality has since declined badly.
→ [Read the full review](/food/laos/luang-prabang/cafes/joma-bakery-cafe/)
