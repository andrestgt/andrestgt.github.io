## Day 3 · Wednesday, 3 June 2020 — Quy Nhơn

*10:11–18:53* — Vietnam20-0230, 0234, 0235, 0236, 0237, 0243, 0244, 0250, 0254, 0258, 0263, 0269

232 Nguyễn Thị Định, Nguyễn Văn Cừ, Quy Nhơn, Bình Định, Vietnam

Brand new Western style café with smooth, well prepared cappuccino for only 39k. Ac and WiFi available but the place is so popular, there's not enough comfy seats

---

**Bánh canh Bà O** — breakfast

384 Bạch Đằng, Thị Nải, Quy Nhơn, Bình Định, Vietnam

This basic hole-in-the-wall eatery is popular for it's Banh Canh noodle soup which is indeed very good here. The star of the show, however, are their Banh Cuon, which here resemble Malaysian popiah and dipped in peanut sauce. Very tasty. Prices are worth it, but quite high for this kind of basic eatery. 52k for a bowl of soup and two rolls

---

**Quy Nhơn Country Club** — lunch drinks

P6P5+W5R, QL1D, Ghềnh Ráng, Quy Nhơn, Bình Định, Vietnam

To call this place quirky would probably be an understatement. It operates on different levels on a hill above Quy Nhon and the views are superb. Decor ranges from old tandem bicycles to boarded up bathtubs as tables. And for some reasons most of their choices of beer come from little known breweries in Southern Germany, though they also serve Tiger. Sadly most German brews were out of stock, but I managed to get a Schwabenbräu Hefeweizen for 100k. Tiger is 30k. I don't think they serve food here which is a bit disappointing, the location would be fantastic for a restaurant

---

**Marina Royale** — afternoon coffee

Possibly the prettiest coffee shop in town. There's ac and garden seating. And it's a lot less expensive than it looks. A Vietnamese coffee is 18k. They also do espresso drinks, but I didn't try any. Small choice of food items

---

**Surf Bar** — afternoon drinks

Location, location. It's a beautiful beach bar with reasonable prices, but stick with beer or coffees. The cocktails are horrible. 30k only for a Tiger in prime location is fair

---

**Quán Ốc Hảo** — snack

18 Ngọc Hân Công Chúa, Trần Phú, Thành Phố, Bình Định, Vietnam

Great variety of shells and mussels at 25k a dish. It's difficult to choose the right dishes though, if you're not an expert. Some are very delicious others rather bland, but at the price you can't really go wrong

---

**Quán Bánh Xèo Tôm Nhảy Rau Mầm** — dinner

91 Đống Đa, Thị Nải, Quy Nhơn, Bình Định, Vietnam

This most unremarkable eatery on one of Quy Nhon's least pleasant roads is not a place where one expects to have a memorable meal, but their 3 types of Banh Xeo (which is all they serve here) are nothing short of spectacular. I have eaten banh xeo all over the country, all kinds of varieties, on the street and in fancy restaurants but nothing comes even close to this perfectly crispy pancake with prawns, squid or marinated beef. Wrapped into rice paper with salad and leaves then dipped into peanut and fish-chili sauce, it's just ridiculously delicious. And it's cheap, a filling meal for 2 is less than 100k, for an incredible culinary experience. If you're in Qui Nhon, you simply must eat here

---

**Quán Nhậu 97** — drinks

In the mostly rather upmarket area around the lake this simple and always packed drinking place stands out for being super welcoming and atmospheric. The local Qui Nhon beer is dirt cheap at 7k a bottle and the food they serve is top notch, well above the usual drinking place average. Especially the grilled dishes are outstanding. Tender octopus, a big portion for only 85k, they also do very tasty fried chicken that goes perfectly with the light Qui Nhon lager. My favourite place in town

**7 Bridges Taproom** — afternoon drinks

79 Lương Hữu Khánh, Thọ Quang, Sơn Trà, Đà Nẵng 550000, Vietnam

When in Da Nang, why not go for the local craft beer microbrewery? Seven Bridges sell all over the country but they're native to Da Nang. Personally I really like their beers and they have very varied selection with a wide range of styles, My Khe Coconut being my favourite. Prices are in line with other tap rooms, not exactly cheap, but they have daily specials and happy hour from 4-7 pm. Nibbles and grub available, too.

For amazing views head upstairs to their terrace, but it pays to reserve if you want the best seats, especially on the weekend.

Their pizzas are the thick crust, cheese overload variety, so they're not for purists. But the Korean mandu pizza is so ridiculously double carbs wrong, it's spectacularly delicious

---

**Ốc Hút 277** — pre-dinner snack

277 Đống Đa, Thạch Thang, Hải Châu, Đà Nẵng 550000, Vietnam

Friendly little whole in the wall which specialises in Oc Hut, the most popular mussel dish in Danang. Accompanied by Mit Tron (jackfruit salad) this is a great pre dinner snack and a must try for all foodies that like to explore local specialties

---

**Hops & Crust - Craft beer and Pizza** — dinner

84 Cao Bá Quát, An Hải Trung, Sơn Trà, Đà Nẵng 550000, Vietnam

Expat Central and a fun place to be, especially when they have live music. Great selection of craft beers on tap, and especially the Belgos are very reasonably priced. There's a happy hour, too, but only for a few select beers. Food wise, they do good pizzas, but be aware that when you order Flammekuche Pizza, you actually do get a pizza, just with Flammekuche toppings, not the actual thin flatbread that I expected. It's super tasty though, so no complaints about that.

### Huế
*14:17–20:45* — Vietnam20-0466, 0469, 0474, 0477, 0480, 0482, 0484, 0488, 0491

**Villa Hue**

4 Trần Quang Khải, tổ 10, Huế, Thừa Thiên Huế, Vietnam

For the price that we got during Covid19 times (400k) the rooms here are fantastic value. Spacious, clean and if they hadn't turned off the mini bar, I'd say minimum 4 star standard. The negative and I don't know if that was due to a lack of staff in crisis times or a general problem was the level of cleanliness in public areas such as the pool and rest rooms. It was almost shocking with overflowing ashtrays, unclean toilets, loo roll lying around in the hallways, almost comical. Having said that, the room itself was absolutely spotless and reception staff was friendly and professional

---

**Hot Tuna** — late lunch

37 Võ Thị Sáu, Phú Hội, Huế, Thừa Thiên Huế, Vietnam

Tourist restaurant in a prime backpacker central location. We were attracted by their German style bia hoi. This being corona time, however, they can't sell fast enough and the cask was off. Still, they do Huda on tap for 18k which is alright. The food is clearly for tourists only and not the kind of quality I'm used to but, you can't fault them for it. The Banh khoai (59k) was perfectly acceptable, just rather unremarkable, and came without a whole lot of herbs and leaves, without banh trang to roll and a rather tame peanut sauce lacking a punch of mam tom and chili.

---

**Cơm hến Hoa Đông** — afternoon snack

Not to be confused with its namesake further down the alley (spot the different accent in the name). ---

**Bún Bò O Ty** — dinner

5 Nguyễn Du, Phú Cát, Huế, Thừa Thiên Huế, Vietnam

There's lots of overpriced tourist bun bo joints in Hue, this is definitely not one of them. A friendly local neighbourhood place which sells excellent bowls for a mere 25k. Well worth finding out before spending double for a mediocre soup on the other side of the river. The broth is very smooth and balanced and the quality of the meat and meat balls is outstanding for such a cheap bowl

---

**DMZ Bar** — evening drinks

60 Lê Lợi, Phú Hội, Huế, Thừa Thiên Huế, Vietnam

This long established backpacker bar is trying to reinvent itself now that there's no more backpackers in the country. Catering to the taste of the local youth means the music is horrible and deafening but despite this and a discount on drinks, it was almost empty on a Sunday night, even with the walking street. In a way it's a sad sight

## Day 8 · Monday, 8 June 2020 — Huế

*09:01–19:00* — Vietnam20-0492, 0496, 0498, 0504, 0505, 0510, 0512, 0514, 0517, 0529, 0543, 0545, 0548, 0555, 0562, 0563

**Cơm Hến Hoa Đông** — breakfast

64/7 64 Kiệt 7 Ưng Bình, Vỹ Dạ, Huế, Thừa Thiên Huế, Vietnam

For a good reason this is considered the best Com Hen (baby mussels rice) place on Hen Island, and it's the most popular too. Prices, however, are still very cheap, a regular bowl is 10k and a bigger bowl with the lot goes for 15k. They also do a Banh Beo platter which puts some of the fancier Hue cake restaurants in town to shame and for the fraction of a price (20k for a platter). I'd highly recommend this place to sample some of Hue's special foods.

---

**Hue Roastery** — morning coffee

Don't be put off by the dark stairway for getting to this second floor coffee shop. Upstairs you'll find a nicely decorated air conditioned space with decent espresso drinks, especially for the price. A well prepared cappuccino costs only 28k, a Vietnamese coffee only 12k. Comfy seating is limited, but the small wooden chairs give you a view over the canal

---

**Gecko** — lunch drinks

This friendly traveller café is still open during corona times and offers really good Huda draft beer for 9k a glass. It's ice cold, fizzy and refreshing, not flat and metallic. Add some decent tourist grub and you can have a good time here. Their Hue cakes are perfectly adequate and good value, and I say that as a food snob. Just stay away from the bun bo Hue which has not much to do with the real thing, but obviously, just like any other noodle soup, it's not a dish you should order in a traveller joint in the first place.

---

**Bún Giấm Nuốc** — snack

2 Đ. Chi Lăng, Phú Cát, Huế, Thừa Thiên Huế, Vietnam

This one is for completists. A rarely found bowl of noodles with jellyfish and prawn crackers apparently only available in summer and in just a handful of places in Hue, so if it's your quest to try all the different noodle dishes in Vietnam, definitely search this one out. It's tasty too. 30k

---

**Bánh Ép Ngon Rẻ** — snack

11 Lê Ngọc Hân, Tây Lộc, Huế, Thừa Thiên Huế, Vietnam

Local speciality street food that looks and tastes a little like Vietnam's answer to a burrito, essentially a pressed pancake wrap with various fillings. Not exactly a culinary revelation, perhaps, but undeniably tasty and dirt cheap.

---

It's a great concept for a beer garden, and I really liked the patatas bravas, the croquetas, the schnitzel and the currywurst. Ironically, you pay less for this kind of food here than you'd pay for local food at some Hanoi local bia hoi joints.

Furbrew is not exactly my favourite craft beer in Vietnam, I prefer Turtle Lake and Seven Bridges, but there's a whopping 21 different beers on tap, and some of them are very good.
