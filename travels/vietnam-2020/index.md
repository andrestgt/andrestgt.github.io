# Vietnam in the Time of Corona

**June – July 2020**

We had originally planned a short bike trip from Nha Trang up to Quy Nhon, three or four days, no more than that. But once we got going, it became clear that this was an unusual moment to be travelling in Vietnam. The country was closed to the outside world because of Covid-19, but inside, everything was still open, and the roads, beaches, caves and mountains were essentially empty of foreign tourists. It was too good an opportunity to pass up.

The short trip turned into an epic 6,600-kilometre loop that took us through most of the country: up the central coast, along the Ho Chi Minh Trail into the far north, around the Hà Giang loop, and back down through the highlands to Nha Trang. Six weeks of riding through the country we thought we knew, with hardly any other tourists on the road.

## 2 June 2020

Next day up to Quy Nhon along the small back roads rather than Highway 1. We first stopped at [Bai Phu Thuong](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=B%C3%A3i+Ph%C3%BA+Th%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng%2C+Ph%C3%BA+Y%C3%AAn) near Ganh Yen, one of the most beautiful beaches in Central Vietnam. It can only be reached through a maze of small village roads, which prevents any development, at least for now. Pristine, clean, with crystal-clear water, a gem of a beach for individual travellers with their own wheels.

The local Quy Nhon beer is 7k a bottle, oysters are fresh and some quite large at 16k a piece, and the bánh tráng with coconut flavour (15k) is perfect with gỏi hải sản (65k).

## 4 June 2020

Early start north. [Vinh Hoi](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=B%C3%A3i+bi%E1%BB%83n+V%C4%A9nh+H%E1%BB%99i%2C+B%C3%ACnh+%C4%90%E1%BB%8Bnh) is one of the nicest beaches in the entire area. When I first visited five years ago, this part of the coast was still very remote, with only small roads and no development. A lot has changed since. Big resorts have been built on some nearby beaches and road extensions have turned large parts of it into construction areas. Vinh Hoi itself is still lovely and has been spared rampant development so far, but it is just a matter of time.

## 25 June 2020

Breakfast at [Phở Lý Thi](https://andrestgt.github.io/food/vietnam/lao-cai/bac-ha/street-food/pho-bac-ha-ly-thi/), a very special place. With many spots in Bắc Hà catering mostly to weekend tourists, this old-school eatery with its communal wooden tables and shelves stacked with home-made rice wine is a very local experience, packed for breakfast even on a regular weekday, with highlanders of various tribes speaking a wild mix of languages. They serve two kinds of phở, using home-made purplish noodles from red rice. The phở chua (sour phở) is a very unusual noodle soup served cold, a strange choice for a cold place like Bắc Hà, and while not at all unpleasant I wasn't entirely taken with its acquired taste. More to my liking was the phở trộn, a dry noodle dish which comes with hot broth on the side and a choice of pork or chicken, which I found very tasty. 20 to 30k a bowl, depending on the meat.

Mid-morning coffee at [Café Sân Vườn 279](https://andrestgt.github.io/food/vietnam/bac-kan/ba-be/cafes/cafe-san-vuon-279/). If we were anywhere south of the northern mountain area, I wouldn't even mention this place. It is pretty much your standard Vietnamese garden café, of which there are usually dozens in most towns further south. Up here, however, this place was an oasis in the desert. Not only are there hardly any cafés at all within 50 kilometres, this one even serves real, good-quality Dak Lak coffee by filter at a low price (20k with or without milk and ice). Plus, it is friendly, with plugs, sofas and Wi-Fi.

The main accommodation options are simple homestays in a small hilltribe village. It is a lovely place, but sadly, while the lake is nice to look at, there is not much to actually do. Boat trips are more geared towards domestic tour groups and quite pricey, and we didn't find any suitable place for a nice swim anywhere around.

The strategic location essentially at the entrance of the best part of the Old Town makes it look like a tourist trap, but it's definitely not. The few dishes they serve are great and prices are very moderate by 2020 Hoi An standards.

Last day of the trip, down from the highlands to Nha Trang.

Morning coffee at [Daklac Coffee](https://andrestgt.github.io/food/vietnam/dak-lak/buon-ma-thuot/cafes/daklac-coffee/), a fancy café with cushioned seats and local art on the walls, where we got a beautiful cappuccino for just 30k and a Vietnamese filter coffee for half.
