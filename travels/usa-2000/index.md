# USA Road Trip
**12 September – 20 October 2000**

A road trip from San Francisco through the American West, followed by island-hopping across Kauai, Molokai, and Oahu in Hawaii.

---

September 12 to October 20

## Day 1 – San Francisco – San Jose – Carmel

Accommodation: Best Western Mission Inn

Good, typically American rooms, though in a rather unappealing location
(219 DM).

## Day 2 – Carmel – Scenic Highway 1 – Big Sur – San Luis Obispo – Santa Barbara

We spent the morning in Carmel, strolling along its beautiful beach and
past its many charming little houses.

trattoria.

Accommodation: Radisson Santa Barbara

Perfectly located right on the beach promenade. The rooms were nothing
special, but very decent (293 DM).

The coastal scenery and beaches here are even more attractive than

hang-out in front of beautiful old houses, a place where some
wonderfully strange characters seem to hang out permanently.

Accommodation: Sheraton Airport

A stylish hotel with comfortable rooms, though unfortunately located
right by the airport in a rather unattractive setting (129 DM).

## Day 4 – Los Angeles

As a city, the sheer sprawl of Los Angeles can easily drive you to
despair. But if you look at L.A. not as a single city but as an entire
region, with its many fascinating places scattered across it, then it
suddenly has an astonishing amount to offer, even far from the coast.
There are magnificent mountain landscapes, Griffith Park, Mulholland
Drive, elegant green residential areas and hip neighbourhoods full of
shops and restaurants, such as Melrose Avenue.

<figure>
  <img src="photos/USA2000-0006.jpg" alt="Los Angeles Mullholland Drive">
  <em>Los Angeles Mullholland Drive</em>
</figure>

There are also pleasant surprises, like discovering that you can eat
extremely well and surprisingly cheaply in Beverly Hills, in what is
essentially an upmarket Chinese canteen.

## Day 5 – Los Angeles

Another fascinating day in L.A., a city that seems to offer an almost
endless variety of things to see. We spent the morning in Old Pasadena
and had an excellent breakfast at Il Piaggi, one of many charming
restaurants housed in the historic buildings lining the main street.

At midday, in unbearable heat, we explored Downtown L.A., where very
different cultures coexist within a relatively small area. There is El
Pueblo, the old town of Los Angeles, with its Mexican market. A few
blocks to the west lies Little Tokyo, where we had excellent and
inexpensive sushi at Frying Fish on the Japanese Village Plaza. East of
El Pueblo is the surprisingly small Chinatown, which consists mainly of
Broadway and a beautiful square set slightly back from the main street.

## Day 6 – Los Angeles – San Diego

The beaches directly south of Los Angeles are nothing particularly
special, mainly because of the industrial facilities spread across the
hinterland. It only really becomes beautiful again once you reach Laguna
Beach, with its wonderful small coves. Further south there are long
sandy beaches with holiday resorts all the way to La Jolla, where
charming little bays once again provide some variety.

We first visited the historic Pueblo in San Diego. Unfortunately, on
Sundays this place is absolute chaos. To make up for it, we enjoyed
excellent, lively Mexican food in a wonderful atmosphere on a large
plaza at Casa de Bandini.

Downtown San Diego is extremely attractive and easy to explore. The old
town and the Gaslamp Quarter are framed by architecturally accomplished
high-rise buildings and offer plenty of outdoor cafés and restaurants,
as well as the remarkable Horton Plaza shopping complex.

<figure>
  <img src="photos/USA2000-0015.jpg" alt="San Diego">

Dinner at the
Star of India in the Gaslamp Quarter was good, though not cheap.

Accommodation: Best Western Bayside Inn

Centrally located, with the usual solid standard of rooms (150 DM).

## Day 7 – Tijuana and San Diego

In the morning we took a trip across the border to Tijuana and were
surprised at how tidy and Americanised the city feels, quite unlike
Nogales, for example, a less frequented Mexican border town. Prices in
Tijuana are not particularly low either, but even here Mexico still
feels like a completely different world.

## Day 11 – Grand Canyon!

When it comes to temple complexes there is Angkor, for towers the Eiffel
Tower, and for bays Halong Bay. The Grand Canyon belongs in exactly the
same category. It is just as indescribably impressive and overwhelming.

The 13-kilometre West Rim Trail is one of the most beautiful walking routes in the world, running mostly right along the edge of the
canyon, with staggering views, and only becoming busy near Grand Canyon
Village.

## Day 12 – Grand Canyon East Rim – Cameron – Page

In the morning we followed the East Rim of the Grand Canyon, enjoying
yet more magnificent views, especially from the Watchtower at Desert
View. We then continued through Native American territory, past a
smaller canyon beyond Cameron, to Page, the only larger settlement in
the area, close to Lake Powell, a huge reservoir spectacularly set into
the landscape.

We had a good Italian dinner at Bella Napoli in Page.

spectacular day of the entire journey. From Page we drove to Monument
Valley, whose otherworldly rock formations can be admired along a 17-mile
unpaved loop road. It would be hard to imagine a more fascinating place

## Day 15 – Panguitch – Zion National Park – St. George

From Panguitch we drove to Zion National Park. Unlike the other great
highlights of the Southwest, Zion is less about bizarre rock formations
or dramatic colours, and more about being a magnificent natural park for
hiking amid an imposing mountain backdrop.

We did two short hikes, both beautiful, and both unbelievably crowded.
The real highlight, however, was the walk at the end of the Riverside
Walk, where the gorge narrows and you can continue straight through the
river, always beneath towering rock plateaus.

barren scenery, until we reached the desert hamlet of Beatty.

From there it is only a few more miles into Death Valley, that
inhospitable, scorching and yet strangely beautiful desert wilderness.

## Day 17 – Lone Pine – June Lake – Mono Lake – Yosemite National Park

We drove across passes and desert valleys to Lone Pine at the foot of a
high mountain range, then continued north via Bishop, where the high
desert gradually turns into alpine high-mountain scenery, and on to June
Lake.

Here we took a particularly scenic detour off the highway, past three
mountain lakes to Mono Lake, with its bizarre rock formations. We then
entered Yosemite National Park via the 3,000-metre-high Tioga Pass,
passing through dramatic alpine landscapes dotted with mountain lakes
edged by sandy beaches, before reaching the western end of the park.

## Day 18 – Yosemite National Park – Mariposa

A spellbinding mountain landscape of valleys, waterfalls and giant sequoia
trees. Unfortunately, the shuttle system here is not yet particularly
well developed, and the sheer volume of car traffic takes away some of
the magic. Added to this is the vast size of the park; it really
deserves much more time, both to escape the crowds in the valley and to
explore it properly.

Nevertheless, the view from Glacier Point was one of the absolute
highlights of the entire journey, and the walk through Mariposa Grove
among the giant sequoias was just as impressive.

## Day 19 – San Joaquin Valley – Santa Cruz – San Jose

In the morning we drove through the rather dull, agricultural San
Joaquin Valley on Route 140 via Merced to Gustine. Only from there did
the road to Gilroy become more scenic and interesting again.

We then headed to the coast and the extremely attractive seaside resort
of Santa Cruz, whose old boardwalk and lively downtown were full of
life.

<figure>
  <img src="photos/USA2000-0071.jpg" alt="Santa Cruz">

Continuing north on Highway 1 along the glorious Pacific coast,
rugged rock landscapes alternate with wonderful secluded coves.

After Pescadero we turned inland via La Honda and drove on to San Jose,
a large city without any particular flair, but by no means unattractive.
The food at Krung Thai was surprisingly good and even reasonably priced.
No wonder we had to queue for a table.

Accommodation: Hyatt San Jose (near the airport)

Comfortable, though not outstanding rooms (\$119).

## Day 20 – San Jose – San Francisco Airport

We drove back from San Jose to San Francisco Airport to return the
rental car. The most exciting moment of the day was realising that we
had left the mobile phone in the car, and that I had to go all the way
back to retrieve it.

Accommodation: Clarion Hotel Millbrae

The airport shuttle worked perfectly and the restaurant was quite
decent. The rooms, however, were rather modest for an airport hotel (204
DM).

## Day 21 – San Francisco – Honolulu – Kauai (Wailua)

A five-hour flight from San Francisco to Honolulu, followed by a
20-minute hop on to Kauai. We picked up our rental car and checked into
our hotel, then spent the evening at the beach and by the pool.

Accommodation: Best Western Plantation Hale, Wailua

A wonderful apartment with two rooms, kitchen, bathroom and a large
veranda, from which you could step straight out to the pool and Jacuzzi.
Not far from the beach, and excellent value for money (192 DM).

## Day 25 – Napali Coast Catamaran Tour

A catamaran tour along the Napali Coast with Holo-Holo Tours. The
excursion was very expensive (\$99), but absolutely worth it: a
seven-hour boat trip including breakfast, lunch, drinks and snorkelling.

The Napali Coast can only be admired by helicopter, boat or partly on
foot. Enormous, bizarrely shaped cliffs rise straight out of the ocean,
interrupted only occasionally by green valleys. At their base are white
sandy beaches, caves and small waterfalls, a breathtaking natural
wonder.
